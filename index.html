Hey, I'm Natalie. I'm a developer, experienced in agile delivery, UX and modern web technologies, with a background in biology and science communication.

I currently work as a front-end developer for BPDTS, providing specialist digital services to the Department for Work and Pensions. I work within an agile squad to transform digital experiences for millions of vulnerable people facing some of the toughest times of their lives.

My team specialise in identity and trust, dealing with citizen information securely and reducing fraud and error.

I originally trained as a biologist with the Dept. of Animal & Plant Sciences at the University of Sheffield. I specialised in the origins of agriculture and even had some of my research published. I love communicating concepts to a wide audience, and so spent a lot of my time teaching and performing outreach.

After graduation, I worked within the science directorate at Kew Gardens in London. I digitised and edited content for Plants of the World Online, a flagship platform to make plant information accessible to the masses.

I then studied with Nortcoders in Manchester to learn Node and the latest technologies for fullstack software development.
